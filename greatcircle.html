CodeKata > GreatCircle

A point on the Earth is defined by latitude and longitude.

The radius of the Earth is approximately 6,371 km.

Latitude and longitude are defined by degrees, minutes, and seconds.

There are 60 minutes in a degree.

There are 60 seconds in a minute.

A latitude is either North or South of the Equator.

A longitude is either East or West of the Greenwich Meridian.

There are 0.017453 radians in a degree.

There are 1.852 kilometres in a nautical mile.

The Haversine Formula gives the great circle distance (d) between two points in terms of latitude in radians (φ) and longitude in radians (λ) where r is the radius of the Earth:

d = 2r arcsin ( sin 2 ( φ 2 - φ 1 2 ) + cos( φ 1 )cos( φ 2 ) sin 2 ( λ 2 - λ 1 2 ) )

Calculate the great circle distance between two given points in both nautical miles and kilometres.
